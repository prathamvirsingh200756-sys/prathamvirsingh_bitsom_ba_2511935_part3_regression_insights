# Final Business Recommendation

## Key Findings

Monthly sales appear most strongly associated with:

- Marketing Spend
- Footfall
- Inventory Availability
- Customer Rating

These variables consistently contribute to stronger sales performance.

---

## Leadership Priorities

Leadership should focus on:

- Improving inventory availability
- Increasing effective marketing investment
- Driving customer traffic
- Maintaining excellent customer experience

---

## Variables to Interpret Carefully

Average Discount Percentage

Large discounts may increase sales while reducing profitability.

Store Type

Differences may reflect market characteristics rather than operational effectiveness.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

- Increase marketing in high-potential stores.
- Improve inventory planning.
- Monitor customer ratings.
- Improve staffing where footfall is high.
- Investigate stores with unusually large residuals.

---

## Risks

Regression identifies statistical relationships rather than direct cause-and-effect relationships.

External influences including seasonality, economic conditions, local competition, and promotional events may affect sales.

Future analysis should include additional explanatory variables to improve prediction accuracy.
